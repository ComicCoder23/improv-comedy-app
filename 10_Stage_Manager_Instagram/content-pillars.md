# Stage Manager — Content Pillars

5 pillars. Scottish content prioritised for first 3 months.

---

## Pillar 1 — Theory & Technique

What it covers: improv principles, formats, games, concepts  
Examples: "Yes, And explained." / "What is a Harold?" / "The difference between short and long form."  
Tone: educational, curator voice, no jargon without definition  
Frequency: 1–2 per week  
Scottish angle: reference how Scottish groups use or teach the technique

---

## Pillar 2 — Show Reviews & Recaps

What it covers: upcoming shows, post-show recaps, notable moments  
Examples: Glasgow Improv Theatre show listings / post-show "what happened" recaps  
Tone: objective, informative, no spoilers for ongoing runs  
Frequency: tied to actual show schedule  
Scottish angle: Central Scotland venues first — Glasgow Improv Theatre, DACBA, local jams

---

## Pillar 3 — Scene Drills

What it covers: specific game or drill breakdowns  
Examples: "The Clap game explained." / "How to play Freeze tag properly."  
Tone: practical, step-by-step, useful for improvisers to share  
Frequency: 1 per week  
Format: numbered steps or simple diagram card

---

## Pillar 4 — Jam Spotlights

What it covers: open jam sessions, beginner nights, drop-in shows  
Examples: DACBA jam listings / Glasgow Improv open nights  
Tone: welcoming, practical (time, venue, cost, level)  
Frequency: event-driven — when jams are scheduled  
Scottish angle: Central Scotland only until account grows

---

## Pillar 5 — Scottish Scene News

What it covers: new groups, festival announcements, cast news, venue changes  
Examples: Bounce House GIT show announcement / EIIF workshop  
Tone: news wire — facts, dates, links where possible  
Frequency: as events happen  
Sources: verified accounts only (see source-map.md)

---

## Content mix target (weeks 1–12)

| Pillar | % of posts |
|---|---|
| Theory & Technique | 30% |
| Drills | 20% |
| Scottish Scene News | 20% |
| Show Reviews & Recaps | 20% |
| Jam Spotlights | 10% |
