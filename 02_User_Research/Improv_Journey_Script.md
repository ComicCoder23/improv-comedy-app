## BEAT ONE

*Three scenes open. They don't connect yet. They will.*

---

### SCENE A — The Hunt

*How do you currently find out what's on in the Central Scotland comedy scene?*

- [ ] Instagram. I follow the right people and hope the algorithm is in a good mood.
- [ ] A WhatsApp group that moves so fast I've missed three shows by the time I scroll back up.
- [ ] Word of mouth. Someone tells someone who tells me, usually the day before.
- [ ] I check Eventbrite like I'm panning for gold.
- [ ] I genuinely don't know. I find out about things after they've happened.
- [ ] I run the thing. I already know. I just wish everyone else did too.

---

### SCENE B — The Block

*What's the biggest thing that gets in the way?*

- [ ] I can't find the information. It's scattered across five platforms and a group chat I've been muted in.
- [ ] I find the information, but too late. The show was last Tuesday.
- [ ] I want to get involved but I don't know where beginners are actually welcome.
- [ ] I'm trying to promote something and it's taking longer than the thing itself.
- [ ] I know people are doing great work but I have no way to show them off or connect them.
- [ ] The fire at Central Station shut everything down for a week and I genuinely had a small breakdown.

---

### SCENE C — The Dream

*If something existed that you'd actually open every week — what would it be?*

- [ ] A calendar. One calendar. All the shows, jams, classes, and workshops. Updated. Reliable. Just... there.
- [ ] A directory. Who's performing, who's teaching, which troupes exist and what they're actually about.
- [ ] A jam finder. I want stage time. Tell me where the open sessions are and when.
- [ ] A beginners guide. A simple path in for people who are terrified and curious in equal measure.
- [ ] A place to post my event without having to do it six times on six different platforms.

## GROUP GAME ONE

*The group game isn't about individuals. It's about the shared experience. The thing everyone in the scene carries.*

*Answer all of these. They are not optional. They are the game.*

---

**1. The Stair.**

*You are heading to The GIT. You are carrying a bag. It might have your laptop in it. Maybe a prop. Maybe just your emotional baggage from a hard week.*

*How do you approach the stair?*

- [ ] Slowly and with full awareness of my own mortality.
- [ ] Confidently until around step four, then much less confidently.
- [ ] I have fallen. I will not say when. I will not say how many times.
- [ ] I have developed a technique. The technique has not been tested by anyone else.
- [ ] I take it as a metaphor for the craft. Every step up is earned.

---

**2. The Ceiling.**

*Middle floor. You stand up too fast, or you forget, or you're mid-conversation about whether the game was found in that last scene.*

*Have you taken a hit?*

- [ ] Yes. Once.
- [ ] Yes. More than once.
- [ ] I am tall. The ceiling and I have an ongoing relationship.
- [ ] I have said "watch your heed" to a taller person and felt briefly powerful.
- [ ] I crouch now. I crouch everywhere. It has changed my posture permanently.

---

**3. Blackfriars, Bell Street.**

*The stage. You've seen it. You might have performed on it. It fits approximately four people if everyone agrees in advance not to move.*

*What is your honest relationship with that stage?*

- [ ] I love it. Small stage, big energy. The audience is basically in the scene with you.
- [ ] I have had a scene fall apart on it and there was nowhere to go. Literally. Nowhere.
- [ ] The intimacy is the point. If you can hold that room you can hold any room.
- [ ] I have never performed on it but I have sweated watching someone else perform on it.
- [ ] It is exactly the right size for the kind of improv that actually matters.

---

**4. Studio 22.**

*The rehearsal space. Technically reachable. The route is a commitment. The climb is a thing that happens to your legs.*

*What does Studio 22 mean to you?*

- [ ] It is where the real work happens. The sweat, the repetition, the breakthroughs nobody else sees.
- [ ] I have arrived there breathless and then had to immediately do a warm-up and I want justice.
- [ ] By the time I get there I'm already in character. A character who is tired.
- [ ] The climb is the first exercise. Whoever designed this did it on purpose.
- [ ] I have not been. I have heard things. I am building up to it.

## GROUP GAME TWO

*For the nerds. You know who you are. You've read the books. You've had the arguments.*

*This one's yours.*

---

**Pick your improv philosophy. No wrong answers. All consequences.**

- [ ] **UCB / The Game** — If you can't name the game, you're not playing it. Find the unusual thing and explore it.
- [ ] **Truth in Comedy / Group Mind** — The scene lives in the space between people. Trust it.
- [ ] **Mick Napier / Pure Chaos** — Have a point of view. Do something. Stop workshopping the scene into nothing.
- [ ] **TJ and Dave / The Obvious** — If the message isn't received, the object doesn't exist. Be present. Be simple. Be true.
- [ ] **The Harold** — Three scenes, a group game, callbacks, convergence. The whole thing is the thing.

*There is no wrong answer. But we will remember yours.*

---

**One more.**

*You are three lines into a scene. Your scene partner says their character keeps their socks in the freezer.*

*You:*

- [ ] Open the freezer. You are curious. You support the world.
- [ ] Ask why. You want to understand the character.
- [ ] Use it as an object. The sock is now a weapon or a clue.
- [ ] Ignore it and introduce something bigger. You are Gallus and you have a plan.
- [ ] Panic internally while maintaining an external calm. This is called acting.
