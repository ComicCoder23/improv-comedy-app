# The Stage Manager's Memory Vault
## Private Excavation Questionnaire — For Your Eyes Only

Answer however you want. Notes, bullet points, voice memo to self, scribble on a napkin. The goal is raw material, not polished answers.

---

### VENUES

1. What's the worst thing that's ever happened to you physically at The GIT? (stairs, ceiling, lighting rig, the bar, a door — doesn't matter)
2. What's the first thing you do when you arrive at The GIT that nobody told you to do — you just learned it?
3. What does it smell like backstage? (honest answer)
4. What's the most ridiculous thing you've ever seen go wrong with the tech/lights/sound at any venue?
5. Which venue has the worst green room situation and why?
6. Where do people actually go after shows? Is there an unwritten rule about this?
7. What's the parking situation joke that everyone who plays The GIT knows without being told?

---

### REHEARSALS

8. Best excuse you've ever been given for someone missing rehearsal.
9. What's the highest number of people who've ever shown up for a full rehearsal — and what was the occasion?
10. What's the lowest? Who was there?
11. What's the "we should rehearse that" thing your group has been saying for literally years and never actually rehearsed?
12. What happens in the first 10 minutes of every rehearsal before anyone actually does anything?
13. Is there a recurring bit that dies in rehearsal and somehow kills at the show?
14. What's the thing that gets improvised in a show that you never rehearsed and it was the best thing you ever did?

---

### TEAM DYNAMICS

15. Who in any group you've been in is always early, always late, or always saying they're "five minutes away"?
16. What's the WhatsApp message that gets posted in the group chat that everyone reads and nobody replies to?
17. What's the group chat called and why?
18. Is there a recurring argument that has never been resolved? (casting, format, warm-up choice, anything)
19. What's the note from a director or teacher that you still think about — good or bad?
20. What's the scene suggestion from an audience that you dread more than any other?
21. What's the suggestion that always gets laughs before the scene even starts?

---

### SHOWS

22. Describe a show that went wrong in a way that was actually funnier than if it went right.
23. What's a character you've played that you know you'll never fully retire?
24. What's the scene offer you made that you immediately regretted the second it left your mouth?
25. Best "yes, and" you've ever been given. Worst one.
26. What's the thing a non-improviser does in a jam that makes every experienced improviser in the room die internally?
27. Has anyone ever walked out? Why?
28. Has anyone ever heckled? What happened?

---

### THE SCENE / CULTURE

29. What's a piece of improv jargon that gets used in Central Scotland that would confuse someone from London?
30. What does "Gallus" actually mean to you personally — not the dictionary definition?
31. Is there an unwritten dress code? What is it?
32. Who's the person every beginner gets pointed to first, and why?
33. What does the Central Station fire story mean to the scene? (your take, not the official version)
34. What's the running joke that's been running so long nobody remembers where it started?
35. What would you never say onstage in a Glasgow improv show that you could say anywhere else?

---

### THE COMMUNITY

36. What's the thing about the Central Scotland scene that you'd never say out loud to a newcomer but it's true?
37. What would you change about it if you had a magic wand and no political consequences?
38. Who's the person in the scene who's never got the recognition they deserved?
39. What keeps people coming back when the admin is a nightmare, the venues are chaotic, and the money doesn't exist?
40. If someone asked you "what is the Central Scotland improv scene?" and you had to answer in one sentence that was also a joke — what would it be?

---

*Take your time with these. Whatever lands hardest, that's what goes in the Harold.*
